The Making of Juvo

Problem

Design and build a web application for charities and foundations to receive donations from people, as well as for people to donate money to charities they find interesting.

Method

I started the development of the fundraising web app by first deciding on a name, logo and color palette. Juvo is the Latin word for 'help'. After deciding on the branding of the web application, I moved onto the discovery phase by creating user stories and user personas. I researched existing fundraising sites such as Kickstarter, JustGiving and GiveForward. Next I compiled user flows and a site map using draw.io. I created wireframes using UXPin. Designing the UI was the next step of the process. I used Illustrator and Photoshop to create the main prototypes. The rest of the pages were designed in the browser. I developed the front-end using Zurb's Foundation Framework with HTML, SASS and jQuery.

Goals
Solution

Create an enjoyable/positive atmosphere

Since some of the fundraising efforts have sad stories to them, I wanted to make the focus of the fundraising effort more positive. I wanted the overall look and feel of the application to have a 'party-type' atmosphere. I chose a teal and yellow palette to help accomplish that goal.

Make it easy to search for causes

On the homepage, I included a search bar for users who already know which causes they want to donate to. For other users who do not have a specific cause in mind, the 'Browse for Charity' page allows users to browse by category. I implmented a filtering capability so that only causes the user is interested are displayed.

Allow fundraiser owner to easily track and maintain their fundraiser

The dashboard was designed to give a quick snapshot of the overall health of a fundraiser. From the dashboard the user has the option to see more detailed statistics and or to update the fundraiser information. There is lots of built in functionality for editing fundraiser info, managing comments and updates.
