The Making of Paycrave

Problem

Wireframe and design a mobile payment app called Paycrave. A user will be able to locate food trucks, order food and pay for their order prior to pick up.

Method to the Madness

I started the development of the Paycrave app by creating user stories. This helped me narrow down which features would be most important to the users. I also researched exisiting apps that had similar goals (Yelp, Square, PayPal mobile). I decided that speed (in locating and ordering) was a major user goal. Since foodtrucks have distinct brands, I felt it important to highlight each food truck's "personality". Secondary goals were to implement a review/feedback system. I used the infomration during my discovery phase to create wireframes. From the wireframes, I created prototypes for various platforms: iOS 7, iPad, Android and Windows Phone. To test out my iOS 7 design I performed a usability test using the prototype web application Marvel. I asked a tester to accomplish some of the main tasks (locating and ordering/paying for a item). From the usability study, I discovered that the tester prefered to have the app make suggestions of causes to donate to based on preferences to narrow down the initial search.

Final Thoughts

This was my first attempt at designing for mobile. I learned the different guidelines for designing for various platforms. I found designing for mobile apps challenging in that there's a lot of information that needs to be displayed with not a lot of real estate. I also learned how usability testing can help the overall UX of an app.
