The Making of Paycrave

Problem

Wireframe and design a mobile payment app called Paycrave. A user will be able to locate food trucks, order food and pay for their order prior to pick up.

Method

I started the development of the Paycrave app by creating user stories. This helped me narrow down which features would be most important to the users. I also researched exisiting apps that had similar goals (Yelp, Square, PayPal mobile). I used the information during my discovery phase to create wireframes. From the wireframes, I created prototypes for various platforms: iOS 7, iPad, Android and Windows Phone. To test out my iOS 7 design I performed a usability test using the prototype web application Marvel. I asked a tester to accomplish some of the main tasks (locating and ordering/paying for a item). From the usability study, I discovered that the tester prefered to have the app make suggestions of causes to donate to based on preferences to narrow down the initial search.

Goals
Solution

User should be able to quickly find and order food items.

One way I accomplished this goal was by avoiding multi-level menus. By including more options/information on a view, the user could minimize the number of delicate maneuvers they would have to perform. In the map view, I tried to include vital information to making a foodtruck selection (distance, review, type of food). I also designed the payment view to require prior credit card entry and minimal inputs, making the payment process fast and simple.

Since foodtrucks have distinct brands, highlight each food truck's "personality".

I allotted a significant amount of space for foodtruck pictures/logos to be displayed. Also when searching for foodtrucks on the map, a short tag line can be displayed per foodtruck, so that the user can get a better idea of what type of food is being offered.

Encourage users to review foodtrucks.

I included several reminders for a user to add a review of the food truck throughout the app. I also designed the review entry page to be minimalistic. I felt that if a user felt submitting a review was simple and not time consuming, the user would be more willing to spend the time to submit a review.
