The Making of Jott.ly

Problem

Create a logo and a landing page for the newly formed startup caleld Jott.ly. It is a simple SaaS (Software as a Service) web application focused on allowing users to sign up and create an account, make simple content pages, and provide a way to collaborate with others within their organization.

Method

The first step in designing the Jott.ly landing page was to understand the product and its intended audience. I interviewed a stakeholder to learn about Jott.ly's overall business goals. I researched similar products such as Dropbox and Google Drive to understand Jott.ly's competitors. With the research I compiled, I began designing a logo and color palette for the company. Once the logo and color palette were decided upon, I created a wireframe of the landing page structure to layout what information was to be displayed and in what order. The next step was to create a protoype using Photoshop. Finally, I coded the landing page and made it landing page responsive.

Goals
Solution

Create a feel of professionalism, yet modern. Minimalistc and clean.

Since Jott.ly is a lightweight web application, I wanted to create a design that mirrored the nature of the application. The message I wanted to get across was that the application uses modern solutions to solve a simple problem. I accomplished this by including modern yet minimalistic elements in the design. The modern color palette of the sea green and orange was chosen. I selected a sans-serif font, to give the product a more modern feel as opposed to using a serif font which usually is associated with a more classic/historic feel. The hexagon used in the logo vs the commonly used circle or square was an attempt to add a more modern element to the design as well.

Make the user feel like they need/want to sign up.

By including a large call to action button as one of the first elements the user sees, I was trying to encourage the user to sign up. I created copy that contained lots of action words such as "Bridge", "Share", "Build" to implicitly encourage users to take action.

User should see a benefit to using our application and understand its value and how simple it is to use.

Through the use of color, space and alignment, I established a visual hierarchy which allows the user to easily grasp the meaning of the product by quickly scanning the landing page. I outlined the major product features with an icon to give the user a visual cue to what type of problems the product can solve.
